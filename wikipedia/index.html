Montserrat Pagan

Pagan in October 2023.[1]

Montserrat Pagan[2] (born May 27, 2002) is an American journalist and writer who studies at New York University. Her Bachelor of Arts double degree is in Comparative Literature and Journalism. She is also completing a minor in Data Science.

She has worked at New York University's Elmer Holmes Bobst Library as a Facilities Operations Assistant since Spring 2023. During her time at NYU, Montserrat has studied abroad in Paris, Accra, and Argentina. She speaks English and Spanish natively. She also speaks and writes in French at an intermediate level.

Biography

Pagan was born in McAllen, Texas. She lived in Fort Worth, Texas from 2009 to 2013. After, Pagan moved to New York City at 11 years old and attended Columbia Secondary School for Math, Science, and Engineering for middle school and high school.

Throughout her adolescence Montserrat Pagan participated in over twelve school and community musical productions on and off stage such as Les Miserables, Nunsense, Hairspray, 9 to 5, and more. She was editor of her high school's weekly newsletter, Columbia Pride, where she reported on culture.

At NYU, Pagan has contributed various articles for university publications like CooperSquared, Washington Square News, and The Violet Edition. Pagan worked for NYU's New York Dance Marathon, which raises money for families battling chilhood cancer, as Photo/Video Chair from May 2023 to April 2024. She produced content for social media and fundraising events.

In Fall 2023, while studying abroad in Buenos Aires, Argentina, Pagan worked for the Buenos Aires Times and wrote articles on politcs and culture. Some coverage includes Argentine President Javier Milei's campaign rallies as well as protests for women's rights.

Pagan has a Youtube channel called Stories Tall about culture, identity, and community in New York City.

Pagan's mother, Nydia Prishker, was born and raised in Reynosa, Mexico. She works as Director of Graduate Education and Assistant Professor of Childhood Education & TESOL at St. Thomas Aquinas College. Pagan's father, José Pagán, was born in Manatí, Puerto Rico. He works at New York University's School of Global Public Health. He is Chair and Professor of the Department of Public Health Policy and Management. Pagan has a fraternal twin sister named Camila and a younger sister named Natalia.
